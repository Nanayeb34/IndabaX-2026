The agent loop is the core iterative cycle that enables an LLM‑based agent to autonomously complete tasks. It begins with **perceive**, where the agent gathers its current knowledge from the conversation history and any tool outputs. Next comes **reason**, where the model generates an internal plan or response, often in the form of a natural‑language plan or a set of tool calls. The **act** phase follows, where the agent performs the chosen actions—such as querying a web search or reading a file. Finally, **observe** allows the agent to ingest the results of those actions, updating its internal state and deciding whether further actions are needed or if it can produce a final answer. Repeating this loop until the goal is satisfied gives the agent flexibility to handle complex, multi‑step tasks without human intervention.

The strength of the loop lies in its ability to combine the LLM’s reasoning with external tools, making the agent both powerful and adaptable while keeping its behavior transparent and controllable.
